Katherine Goble Johnson

Katherine Goble Johnson was born on August 26, 1918, in White Sulphur Spring, West Virginia. During childhood, she showed advanced skills in the area of mathematics and attended high school at the age of 10. Johnson graduated from West Virginia State College in 1937 with a bachelor's degree in Mathematics and French. Johnson was a mathematician who calculated and analyzed flight paths of spacecraft that helped send astronauts to the Moon. Johnson received numerous awards for her work including the Presidental Medal of Freedom in 2015 presented by President Barack Obama.

Dorothy Vaughan

Dorothy Vaughan was born on September 20, 1910, in Kansas City, Missouri. Vaughan graduated from Wilberforce University in 1929 with a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics. Early in her career, she worked as a math teacher in Virginia public schools. Vaughan made essential contributions with calculations to the early US space program. Vaughan was the first African American manager at the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA) which later became part of NASA. Vaughan died on November 10, 2008, in Hampton, Virginia.

Mary Jackson

Mary Jackson was born on April 9, 1921, in Hampton, Virginia. Jackson was a graduate of Hampton University in 1942 with a dual degree in Mathematics and Physical Science. At the beginning of her career, Jackson was a math teacher in Maryland public schools before returning home to Hampton, Virginia. In 1958, Jackson became the first African American female aerospace engineer to work at NASA. Jackson died on February 11, 2005, in Hampton, Virginia.
